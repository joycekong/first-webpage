What Do House Rabbits Eat?

1 . Hay! Hay! Hay!

Hay keeps the rabbit's gut moving and flushes out hair from grooming; without hay, rabbit gets GI stasis and dies. Hay grinds the rabbit's teeth, which continually grow and must have daily grinding with hay. Grass on a lawn could be okay so long as there is no pesticides or fertilizer sprayed on it. House rabbits should not be left to graze on an open lawn as they can fall prey to large birds and cats.


Types of Hay:
  • Timothy (First Cut) - higher in fiber, course (good for gut stimulation and teach grinding).
  • Timothy (Second Cut) - softer, leafier, more fragrant.
  • Orchard - similar nutritional value to Timothy but different texture.
  • Alfafa - good for rabbits less than 1 year old since protein and calcium rich.
  • Oat - fragrant, good treat hay for rabbits of all ages.



Remember: Rabbits need to have an ALL-YOU-CAN-EAT HAY BUFFET 24/7!

3. Pellets

Give 1/4 cup per 5 lb rabbit daily.

4. Treats

Ideal treats: Dried apple tree sticks, pear tree sticks, wicker, willow sticks are some other items on which the rabbit can grind their teeth. Make sure to get sticks that have not been treated with dies, wax or pesticides. Less Ideal treats/sweets: apple, pear, peach, strawberry, banana, carrot, cherry, plum, kiwi, papaya, mango, pineapple, melon with peel, starfruit, apricot, currants, nectarine, raspberries, blueberries, blackberries, citrus
